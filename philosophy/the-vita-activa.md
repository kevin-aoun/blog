# The Vita Activa

> Dummy note — placeholder content to show a second page nesting under the
> same section.

Arendt splits the active life into three:

| Activity | Corresponds to | Roughly |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Labor | biological necessity | survival, consumption |
| Work | the artificial world | making durable things |
| Action | plurality | politics, speech, beginnings |

The interesting move is that modernity, she argues, inverts the old hierarchy
and elevates labor — turning humans into *animal laborans*.

# Arendt on Action

> matter block at the top and you're good.

For Arendt, *action* is the one human activity that happens directly between
people, without the mediation of things. It's where we disclose *who* we are
rather than merely *what* we are.

## Why it matters

- Action is unpredictable — once begun, it can't be undone or controlled.
- It depends on plurality: a world of others to act with and be seen by.
- This is the root of both freedom and fragility in political life.

## Open questions

1. How does this hold up in a world mediated almost entirely by *things*
   (screens, platforms, algorithms)?
2. Is "disclosure of the self" still possible at scale, or only in small
   publics?
